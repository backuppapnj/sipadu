## 1. Pengadilan Agama Organizational Structure in Indonesia

### Three-Tier Judicial Hierarchy

The Indonesian religious court system (Peradilan Agama) operates under a three-tier hierarchy established by the Constitution (UUD 1945, Article 24) and governed by Law No. 7/1989 (as amended by Law No. 3/2006 and Law No. 50/2009):

1. **Mahkamah Agung (MA)** — Supreme Court. Oversees all judicial branches. The **Direktorat Jenderal Badan Peradilan Agama (BADILAG)** is the directorate general specifically managing religious courts under MA.

2. **Pengadilan Tinggi Agama (PTA)** — Provincial-level appellate Religious High Courts. Each province has one PTA. PA Penajam falls under **PTA Samarinda** (East Kalimantan). PTA functions include:
   - **Pembinaan** (guidance): Technical and administrative direction to PAs in its jurisdiction
   - **Pengawasan** (supervision): Oversight of judges, registrars, and bailiffs
   - **Nasehat** (advisory): Legal opinions on Islamic law for government agencies
   - **Administratif**: Finance, personnel, and general administration

3. **Pengadilan Agama (PA)** — District/city-level first-instance Religious Courts. They examine, adjudicate, and decide cases in matters of marriage, inheritance, wills, gifts (hibah), waqf, zakat, infaq, shadaqah, and sharia economics.

### Key Roles within a Pengadilan Agama

| Role | Indonesian Title | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Chief Judge | Ketua PA | Court leadership, case assignment, institutional management |
| Deputy Chief Judge | Wakil Ketua PA | Assists Ketua, acts in Ketua's absence |
| Judges | Hakim | Examine and adjudicate cases in panels of 3 |
| Registrar | Panitera | Head of court registry (Kepaniteraan), manages all case administration |
| Deputy Registrar | Wakil Panitera | Assists Panitera |
| Substitute Registrar | Panitera Pengganti | Serves as court clerk during hearings |
| Secretary | Sekretaris | Head of Secretariat, manages general administration |
| Bailiff | Jurusita / Jurusita Pengganti | Serves summons, executes court orders |
| PTSP Officers | Petugas PTSP | Front-line service officers at the one-stop service counter |
| Legal Aid Post | Petugas Posbakum | Provides free legal aid information to underprivileged litigants |

### Supervisory Bodies

- **Badan Pengawasan Mahkamah Agung** — Internal supervision body of the Supreme Court
- **Komisi Yudisial** — External body overseeing judge conduct
- **SIWAS (Sistem Informasi Pengawasan)** — MA's online complaint/whistleblowing application for reporting misconduct

## 2. PA Penajam Specific Context

### Basic Information

- **Official name**: Pengadilan Agama Penajam
- **Location**: Jl. Provinsi KM. 9, Komplek Perkantoran, Kec. Penajam, Kab. Penajam Paser Utara, Kalimantan Timur 76142
- **Website**: [pa-penajam.go.id](https://pa-penajam.go.id/)
- **Contact**: 0822-5693-1508
- **Parent PTA**: PTA Samarinda (Kalimantan Timur)
- **Court class**: Likely Kelas II (smaller jurisdiction)

### History

PA Penajam was relatively recently established. It was previously a branch office (cabang) of PA Tanah Grogot, operating from Kompleks Islamic Center Kilometer 8, Kelurahan Nipah-Nipah, Kecamatan Penajam, initially only one day per week. The formal establishment as an independent court was launched circa November 2017.

### Jurisdictional Context

Kabupaten Penajam Paser Utara (PPU) is a relatively small regency in East Kalimantan, adjacent to Balikpapan. It is part of the broader Ibu Kota Nusantara (IKN) development zone, which may significantly increase future caseload and service demands.

## 3. Complaint Types Specific to Pengadilan Agama

### 3.1 Administrative Complaints (Pengaduan Administrasi)

- Errors in case documents (akta cerai, salinan putusan)
- Delays in document issuance
- Incorrect data entry in case records
- Problems with case registration process
- Billing/cost disputes (panjar biaya perkara)

### 3.2 Case Delay Complaints (Keterlambatan Penanganan Perkara)

- Cases exceeding the 5-month standard resolution timeframe (per SEMA guidelines)
- Delayed scheduling of hearings
- Prolonged mediation processes without resolution
- Delays in sending case files for appeal (minutasi)
- Slow execution of court decisions (eksekusi)

### 3.3 Officer Conduct Complaints (Perilaku Pegawai)

- Unprofessional behavior by court staff
- Alleged corruption, collusion, or nepotism (KKN)
- Solicitation of unofficial fees (pungutan liar / pungli)
- Discrimination in service delivery
- These are typically reported through **SIWAS** (Badan Pengawasan MA) or the court's internal complaint mechanism

### 3.4 PTSP Service Complaints (Pengaduan Layanan PTSP)

- Long waiting times at service counters
- Inadequate information provided to litigants
- Incomplete or unclear service procedures
- Issues with e-Court / e-Litigasi registration
- Problems with online payment processing

### 3.5 Information Access Complaints (Pengaduan Akses Informasi)

- Difficulty accessing case status information
- Non-responsive information desk
- Incomplete court website information
- Issues with SIPP data accuracy
- Under Law No. 14/2008 on Public Information Openness (KIP), courts must provide information access

### 3.6 Marriage/Divorce Administration Complaints

- Delays in issuance of Akta Cerai (divorce certificate)
- Problems with Cerai Talak (husband-initiated divorce) processing
- Problems with Cerai Gugat (wife-initiated divorce) processing
- Issues with marriage dispensation (dispensasi nikah) processing
- Complications with isbat nikah (marriage validation) cases
- Problems accessing Posbakum (legal aid post) services
- Issues with prodeo (fee-waived) case applications for underprivileged litigants

## 4. SK KMA No. 026/KMA/SK/II/2012 — Court Service Standards

### Overview

Surat Keputusan Ketua Mahkamah Agung RI Nomor 026/KMA/SK/II/2012 (dated February 9, 2012) establishes the **Standar Pelayanan Peradilan** (Judicial Service Standards) applicable across all four court branches and all court levels.

### Purpose

1. Improve court service quality for justice seekers and the public
2. Increase public trust in the judiciary
3. Serve as a commitment from courts to deliver quality service
4. Provide benchmarks for the public to assess court service quality
5. Serve as a reference for each work unit (satuan kerja) in organizing services

### Scope

Applies to first-instance courts and appellate courts across all four judicial branches:
- Badan Peradilan Umum (General Courts)
- **Badan Peradilan Agama (Religious Courts)**
- Badan Peradilan Tata Usaha Negara (Administrative Courts)
- Badan Peradilan Militer (Military Courts)

### Key Service Standards

The SK mandates that each court unit must develop and publish service standards (standar pelayanan) covering:
- **Service requirements** (persyaratan pelayanan)
- **Service procedures** (prosedur/mekanisme pelayanan)
- **Service timeline** (jangka waktu pelayanan)
- **Service costs** (biaya/tarif pelayanan)
- **Service products** (produk pelayanan)
- **Complaint handling mechanisms** (penanganan pengaduan)

### Complaint Handling Requirements

Courts must provide:
- Clear complaint submission channels
- Defined response timelines for complaints
- Transparent complaint resolution procedures
- Feedback mechanisms for complainants

### Reference Documents

- [JDIH Mahkamah Agung — SK KMA No. 26/KMA/SK/II/2012](https://jdih.mahkamahagung.go.id/index.php/legal-product/sk-kma-nomor-26kmaskii2012/detail)

## 5. PTSP — Pelayanan Terpadu Satu Pintu

### Legal Basis

Keputusan Direktur Jenderal Badan Peradilan Agama Nomor **1403.b/DJA/SK/OT.01.3/8/2018** (August 2, 2018) — Guidelines for PTSP Standards in Religious Courts.

### Principles

PTSP operates on five principles:
1. **Keterpaduan** (Integration)
2. **Efektif, Efisien, Ekonomis** (Effective, Efficient, Economical)
3. **Koordinasi** (Coordination)
4. **Akuntabilitas** (Accountability)
5. **Aksesibilitas** (Accessibility)

### Service Categories at PTSP

| No | Service Category | Description |
|----|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Pendaftaran Perkara | Case registration (gugatan, permohonan, upaya hukum, konsignasi, eksekusi) |
| 2 | Pembayaran Biaya | Fee estimation (SKUM), PNBP payment, refund of surplus deposits |
| 3 | Informasi dan Pengaduan | Information desk and complaint handling |
| 4 | Penyerahan Produk Pengadilan | Delivery of court products (salinan putusan, akta cerai, etc.) |
| 5 | E-Court dan E-Litigasi | Online case registration, payment, and summons |
| 6 | Layanan Hukum Masyarakat Tidak Mampu | Fee waivers (prodeo), mobile court (sidang keliling), Posbakum |

### PTSP Workflow

1. Visitor takes a queue number
2. Visitor presents required documents to PTSP officer
3. PTSP officer verifies, records, and forwards documents to back-office
4. Back-office processes per applicable SOP
5. Result/product returned to visitor via PTSP counter

## 6. SIPP — Sistem Informasi Penelusuran Perkara

### Overview

SIPP (Case Tracking Information System) is a web-based application developed by Mahkamah Agung for court case administration and public tracking. It is the primary case management system used across all Indonesian courts.

### Access Points

- **Central portal**: [sipp-ma.mahkamahagung.go.id](https://sipp-ma.mahkamahagung.go.id/)
- **Per-court portals**: Each court has its own SIPP instance, typically at `sipp.pa-[city].go.id`
- **PA Penajam SIPP**: Expected at `sipp.pa-penajam.go.id`

### Capabilities

- Case registration and tracking
- Hearing schedule management (jadwal sidang)
- Case status monitoring (for public and internal use)
- Case timeline tracking
- Integration with e-Court for online registration
- Integration with Direktori Putusan for published decisions

### Data Organization

Cases in SIPP are organized by:
- **Case type**: Gugatan (contentious), Permohonan (voluntary/non-contentious)
- **Case category**: Cerai Talak, Cerai Gugat, Isbat Nikah, Dispensasi Nikah, Waris, Waqf, etc.
- **Case number format**: e.g., `123/Pdt.G/2026/PA.Pnj` (Pnj = Penajam)
- **Status**: Proses (ongoing), Minutasi, Putus (decided), Upaya Hukum (appeal)

### Related Systems

| System | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **e-Court** | Online case registration and payment |
| **e-Litigasi** | Online litigation (hearings, document submission) |
| **Direktori Putusan** | Public repository of court decisions (putusan3.mahkamahagung.go.id) |
| **SIWAS** | Complaint and whistleblowing system under Badan Pengawasan MA |

### Integration Considerations for SIPADU

SIPADU may need to reference SIPP case numbers when complaints are related to specific cases. However, direct SIPP database integration is unlikely to be permitted. Options include:
- Manual case number entry by complainants
- Read-only lookups via SIPP's public-facing pages (scraping is fragile and not recommended)
- Formal API access request to BADILAG (unlikely for a single PA-level system)

## 7. SP4N-LAPOR! Integration Requirements

### Overview

SP4N-LAPOR! (Sistem Pengelolaan Pengaduan Pelayanan Publik Nasional — Layanan Aspirasi dan Pengaduan Online Rakyat) is Indonesia's centralized national complaint management platform, accessible at [lapor.go.id](https://www.lapor.go.id).

### Legal Mandate

- **Perpres No. 76/2013**: Establishment of LAPOR! as the national complaint management system
- **PermenPAN-RB No. 3/2015**: Operational guidelines
- **Perpres No. 95/2019**: **Mandates all government agencies** (including courts) to integrate with SP4N-LAPOR!

### Managing Institutions

- **KemenPAN-RB** (Ministry of Administrative and Bureaucratic Reform) — Public service supervisor
- **KSP** (Presidential Staff Office) — National priority program supervisor
- **Ombudsman RI** — Public service oversight

### Current Coverage

SP4N-LAPOR! is connected to:
- 34 Ministries
- 96 Government Institutions
- 493+ Local Governments
- Channels: website (lapor.go.id), SMS 1708, Twitter @lapor1708, mobile apps (Android/iOS)

### Complaint Flow in SP4N-LAPOR!

1. Citizen submits complaint via any channel
2. Complaint is classified and routed to the responsible institution
3. Institution receives, verifies, and processes the complaint
4. Institution responds within the mandated timeframe
5. Citizen can track status and provide feedback
6. Complaint is closed upon resolution

### Integration Requirements for SIPADU

Based on the Perpres mandate, PA Penajam should be connected to SP4N-LAPOR!. Integration approaches:

**Option A: Manual Sync (Minimum Compliance)**
- Complaints received via LAPOR! are manually entered into SIPADU by designated officers
- SIPADU complaint resolutions are manually updated back to LAPOR!
- Pros: Simple, no technical dependency
- Cons: Labor-intensive, risk of data inconsistency, delays

**Option B: API Integration (Preferred)**
- Technical API documentation for SP4N-LAPOR! is **not publicly available**
- API access is provided to authorized government agencies through formal coordination with KemenPAN-RB
- Based on IEEE-published research, SP4N-LAPOR! does support API-based integration with "client apps" (institution-level complaint systems)
- Integration typically involves:
  - Complaint data push/pull via REST API
  - Status synchronization between local system and LAPOR!
  - Webhook notifications for new complaints assigned to the institution

**Option C: Embed/Link Approach (Pragmatic)**
- SIPADU provides a link/redirect to LAPOR! for complaints that fall under SP4N jurisdiction
- SIPADU focuses on internal PA-specific complaints
- Periodic manual reconciliation

### Technical Considerations

- SP4N-LAPOR! API specs must be requested through official KemenPAN-RB channels
- The integration typically requires a formal MoU between the institution and KemenPAN-RB
- Data format alignment (complaint categories, status codes) must match SP4N standards
- Authentication likely uses institution-level API keys

### Recommended Approach for SIPADU

Given that PA Penajam is a small Kelas II court:
1. **Phase 1**: Build SIPADU as a standalone internal complaint system
2. **Phase 2**: Implement manual sync with SP4N-LAPOR! via officer workflow
3. **Phase 3**: If/when API access is granted, implement automated bidirectional sync
